## Abstract

Moiré patterns — frequency aliasing artifacts from camera-screen interference — remain a persistent challenge in computational photography. While deep learning methods achieve strong results on trained device-screen combinations, their performance collapses when deployed on unseen capture setups, owing to severe domain overfitting. We propose MoTA (Moiré Test-Time Adaptation), a model-agnostic framework that enables any pre-trained demoiréing backbone to adapt to unseen domains at inference time without paired target-domain data. MoTA introduces two complementary lightweight adapters — a Fourier Domain Adapter and a Spatial Gating Adapter — that together account for less than 5% of backbone parameters, and a Moiré-Aware Self-Supervision (MASS) signal that generates pseudo-clean targets via selective frequency-band attenuation. On cross-domain benchmarks spanning three unseen dataset distributions, MoTA reduces the domain gap by up to *X* dB PSNR compared to standard frozen inference, while maintaining competitive in-domain performance. The adapter design is architecture-agnostic: we demonstrate consistent gains when plugging MoTA into CNN, Transformer, and wavelet-based backbones.
